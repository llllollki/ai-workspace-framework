## Phase 1 Partner Focus

Business development should focus on assisted living facilities in and around these hospital-centered launch markets:

- Temecula Valley Hospital
- Inland Valley Hospital
- Rancho Springs Hospital
- Loma Linda University Medical Center - Murrieta
- Menifee Global Medical Center

Priority targets:

- RCFE and elderly residential care communities in the supported service area
- facilities with strong responsiveness and operational follow-through
- facilities that want additional local family visibility
- facilities that can benefit from scheduling support for calls and tours

## Business Objective

Build a repeatable local-market partner network of facilities that:

- pay for listing visibility and participation
- may purchase premium operational and marketing add-ons
- respond to qualified family opportunities in a timely way
- support a high-trust family experience

The commercial model should be designed to:

- generate recurring revenue
- support partner retention
- reward responsiveness and service quality
- preserve trust with families

## Core Partner Value Proposition

We help local assisted living communities connect with families who are actively looking for support in their area.

Our value is not just traffic. We:

- attract local families who are actively exploring care
- gather intake information to understand basic fit
- help connect families with relevant facilities
- help coordinate calls and visits when appropriate
- give facilities stronger local visibility in a focused market

## Commercial Guardrails

All sales and marketing language should remain conservative and compliance-aware.

Use language like:

- qualified local family inquiries
- local visibility
- community profile exposure
- scheduling support
- placement support
- matching support
- family coordination support

Avoid language like:

- guaranteed move-ins
- guaranteed occupancy
- guaranteed placement
- exclusive access to all leads
- pre-approved families
- medically cleared residents
- hospital-affiliated placement service

Additional guardrails:

- clearly disclose when placement is sponsored, featured, or prioritized
- do not imply hospital endorsement unless true
- do not promise live availability unless verified
- do not suggest that payment overrides care fit or safety considerations
- do not promise a specific lead volume unless contractually defined

## Recommended Package Structure

The initial pricing model should be simple and easy to explain.

Recommended structure:

1. Starter Listing
2. Growth Listing
3. Premium Concierge Add-On

This keeps the base sale straightforward while allowing upsell without making the first conversation too complex.

## Pricing Tiers

These are recommended starting points for early market testing. Pricing can be adjusted after learning from real sales conversations.

### Starter Listing

Recommended price:

- `$299` to `$499` per month per facility

Recommended fit:

- facilities that want local visibility and access to the matching ecosystem
- facilities that are testing the service for the first time

Includes:

- standard facility listing
- basic profile page
- eligibility to appear in relevant local matches
- standard community contact display
- basic reporting on lead activity
- standard account support

Compliant value statement:

Starter gives your community a paid presence in our local family discovery and matching network. It is designed to increase relevant local visibility and help your community be considered when families are exploring options in your market.

### Growth Listing

Recommended price:

- `$699` to `$999` per month per facility

Recommended fit:

- facilities that want stronger visibility and a more competitive market presence
- facilities that value enhanced profile quality and local prominence

Includes everything in Starter, plus:

- enhanced facility profile
- expanded photos and profile content
- stronger placement in approved featured areas
- priority visibility within defined consumer experiences
- enhanced reporting and partner insights
- faster standard support response

Compliant value statement:

Growth is designed for communities that want a stronger presentation and more prominent visibility within clearly disclosed placement opportunities. Priority visibility should always operate within trust-preserving rules and should not override core fit and safety considerations.

### Premium Concierge Add-On

Recommended price:

- `$500` to `$1,500` per month add-on

Alternative pricing option:

- monthly base plus a per-scheduled-tour service fee

Recommended fit:

- facilities that want added help coordinating inquiries, calls, and visits
- facilities that want more hands-on support moving interested families toward next steps

Includes:

- scheduling coordination support
- follow-up help for calls and tours
- operational assistance with family communication handoffs
- rescheduling support where appropriate
- more hands-on account attention

Compliant value statement:

The Concierge Add-On provides additional coordination support intended to reduce operational friction between family inquiry and a scheduled next step. It does not guarantee a tour, admission, or move-in.

## Sales Pitch

### Core Positioning

We help assisted living facilities in your market connect with families who are actively looking for support, and we help reduce the friction between lead inquiry and a scheduled call or visit.

### Short Pitch

We work with families in the Temecula, Murrieta, Menifee, and surrounding markets who are actively searching for assisted living support. Instead of just sending cold directory traffic, we qualify families, understand their care needs, and help coordinate the next step. Our goal is to connect your community with better-fit families and help move them toward scheduled calls and tours faster.

### Sales Call Version

We are building a local assisted living matching and placement support platform focused on families who need help finding care in your area. When families come to us, we guide them through a short intake, learn what kind of care and location they need, and match them with facilities that may be a good fit.

For our facility partners, we offer a paid listing that puts your community into that local matching flow. We also offer premium options like scheduling support for calls and visits, stronger profile visibility, priority placement, and more robust community profiles.

The value is not just exposure. We help reduce the back-and-forth by qualifying families, coordinating communication, and helping move interested families toward real conversations and tours.

### Why A Facility Should Care

- access to qualified local families instead of low-intent general traffic
- better visibility in a focused local market
- operational help with scheduling calls and tours
- improved response handling when your staff is busy
- optional premium positioning and profile enhancements

### Sales Principles

- keep the pitch local and market-specific
- sell qualified family connection, not just traffic

## Objection Handling

### "We already get referrals."

Recommended response:

That makes sense. Our value is not just another name on a list. We are trying to bring in more qualified local families and help reduce friction by supporting scheduling and follow-up.

### "We do not want to pay for leads."

Recommended response:

This is not positioned as a pure lead-broker model. The core package is a paid local listing and visibility product, with optional support services that can help your team handle family coordination more efficiently.

### "Can you guarantee move-ins?"

Recommended response:

No. We do not guarantee admissions, occupancy, or move-ins. What we provide is local visibility, qualified family matching support, and optional scheduling assistance to help create more opportunities for meaningful conversations and tours.

### "Can we be listed first?"

Recommended response:

We do offer enhanced and featured placement opportunities, but those placements should be clearly disclosed and operate within trust-preserving rules. They do not override core fit and matching standards.

## Business Development Workflow

### Partner Acquisition Workflow

1. Identify target facilities within each Phase 1 launch market
2. Prioritize top facilities based on fit, local reputation, responsiveness, and service coverage
3. Reach out by phone, email, and other business development channels
4. Track interest, objections, and follow-up activity
5. Convert facilities into paid listing partners
6. Onboard the partner and configure their listing and service package
7. Monitor lead flow, responsiveness, and upsell opportunities

### Partner Onboarding Workflow

The onboarding process should capture:

- business contact and billing contact
- facility contact methods
- preferred intake and scheduling workflow
- listing tier
- premium add-ons selected
- profile content and media
- service-area and care-fit details
- contract status
- billing status

### Partner Success Workflow

After onboarding, the team should track:

- lead volume
- response speed
- scheduling conversion
- tour completion
- move-in outcomes where available
- renewal likelihood
- upsell opportunities

## Recommended Contract Concepts

Final agreements should be reviewed by counsel, but commercially the contract should address:

- package tier

- add-ons selected
- billing terms
- renewal terms
- cancellation terms
- permitted use of family lead information
- restrictions on reuse or resale of lead data
- disclosure rules for premium visibility
- service limitations and no-guarantee language

## Recommended Launch Offer

For initial market entry, consider a simple introductory offer:

- reduced first-month or first-two-month rate
- waived setup fee for early adopters
- discounted upgrade from Starter to Growth in the first 60 days

Compliant framing:

- introductory local market launch pricing
- founding partner rate
- early partner package

Avoid:

- limited guaranteed lead package
- guaranteed occupancy launch offer

- monthly recurring revenue
- average revenue per partner

- renewal rate
